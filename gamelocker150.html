GameLocker150

HTML5 Game Developer

Fall 2014 (2 months) | Lehigh University

GameLocker150 is a set of web games developed for the promotion of the Lehigh University/Lafayette College football rivalry.

Description

I developed the "Swimming" HTML5 Game using Construct 2. Our team collaborated with the Fusion Design Studio to create original art content. I was the only high school member of the team and took the project from ideation at the initial brainstorming meeting to execution. We decided to create a collection of mini-games to have Lehigh students "compete" against Lafayette students in common athletic events, including football, basketball, and swimming. I conceived of swimming mini-game with a "Flappy Bird" style. I started with low-fidelity paper-and-pen sketches and communicated my vision to the designers for their art production. Throughout the process of development and user testing, I added features, such as power-ups and a progressively increasing speed for the oncoming swimmers to make the game more challenging. I also initially thought of having subtle moving bubbles in the background to mimic movement, but we removed this feature to maintain a consistent, flat design across games. Our work was featured in articles and reached hundreds of users.

Articles about the project were published by the Brown and White, LehighValleyLive, and Lehigh.edu.
